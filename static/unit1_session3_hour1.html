Unit 1 – People

Session 3

People you know

1st Hour · Grammar & Writing

Focus: Wh- questions · adjectives · short descriptions

Objectives

By the end of this hour, students can:
  • Use Wh- questions to ask about people.
  • Use adjectives to describe personality and appearance.
  • Write a short description of a real person they know.
  • Review verb be in context.

Warm-up · People in your life

Think of three important people in your life.

  • Who are they?
  • How old are they?
  • What are they like?
Example: “This is my friend Laura. She is 28. She is very friendly and organized.”

Grammar · Wh- questions

Review – asking about people

Question word Meaning / use Example
Who Person Who is he?
Where Place / origin Where is she from?
How old Age How old is he?
What … like Personality / character What is she like?
What Job What does he do?
Complete the questions:
1. ___ is she?
2. ___ is he from?
3. ___ old is your friend?
4. ___ is she like?
5. ___ does he do?

Adjectives to describe people

Personality

  • friendly
  • quiet
  • serious
  • funny
  • talkative
  • kind
  • shy
  • confident
  • patient
  • energetic

Appearance

  • tall
  • short
  • young
  • old
  • strong
Use be:
“She is tall.” – NOT “She has tall.”

Controlled practice

Complete with adjectives:

  • friendly · funny · quiet · tall · young

1. My nephew is very ______.
2. My grandmother is ______ and ______.
3. My best friend is ______ and ______.
4. My teacher is ______ but very ______.

Optional: ask students to read one sentence aloud.

Guided writing · Model text

Read the model description:

“This is my friend Marco. He is 25 years old.
He is from Spain. He is Spanish.
He is tall and very friendly.
He works in a café and he is very funny.”

Guided writing · Your turn

Write about a real person you know:

  • Who is the person?
  • How old are they?
  • Where are they from?
  • What are they like? (2–3 adjectives)
  • What do they do?

Write 4–6 sentences in your notebook.

Then, work in pairs and read your description to your partner.

Wrap-up

  • Ask 2–3 students to share one sentence about their person.
  • Highlight good use of adjectives and Wh- questions.
  • Check pronunciation of key words: friendly, quiet, serious, talkative.

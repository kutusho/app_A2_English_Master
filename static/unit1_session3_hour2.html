Unit 1 – People

Session 3

People you know

2nd Hour · Listening & Speaking

Extra slow listening · descriptions · partner work

Objectives

By the end of this hour, students can:
  • Understand slow descriptions of different people.
  • Identify name, age, personality and job from audio.
  • Repeat and practise key adjectives with clear pronunciation.
  • Describe a real person and share their description orally.

Listening 1 · Welcome

Audio: U1_S3_audio1_intro.mp3

Extra slow · warm introduction to Session 3 listening.

  • Play the audio once without stopping.
  • Ask: What are we going to do in this part of the class?
  • Elicit: listen to descriptions, focus on name, age, personality, job.

Listening 2 · Adjectives drill

Audio: U1_S3_audio2_adjectives_drill.mp3

Extra slow repetition of key adjectives.

Adjectives:

  • friendly, funny, quiet, serious, talkative
  • kind, shy, confident, patient, energetic
Procedure:
1) Students listen only.
2) Students listen and repeat in chorus.
3) Optional: individual students repeat one adjective.

Listening 3 · Short descriptions

Audio: U1_S3_audio3_short_descriptions.mp3

Three mini-descriptions – listen for specific information.

Students complete the table while listening:

# Name Age Personality Job
1 Maria ________ ________ ________
2 Roberto ________ ________ ________
3 Elena ________ ________ ________
Play twice: first for general idea, second for details.

Listening 4 · Long description

Audio: U1_S3_audio4_long_description.mp3

Extra slow · longer, more natural description.

After listening, ask:

  • How old is Daniel?
  • What is his job?
  • What adjectives describe him?
Option: play again and ask students to underline the adjectives they hear.

Listening 5 · Final task instructions

Audio: U1_S3_audio5_final_task.mp3

Extra slow instructions for partner activity.

Students follow these steps:

  1. Think of a person they know well.
  2. Ask their partner:
    • Who is the person?
    • How old is he or she?
    • What is he or she like?
    • What does he or she do?
  3. Listen carefully and take notes.
  4. Introduce their partner’s person to the class.

Speaking · Introduce a person

Model structure for students:

“This is my partner’s friend Ana. She is 30 years old.
She is from Mexico. She is very friendly and energetic.
She works as a designer.”

  • Students present in pairs or small groups.
  • Teacher listens for correct use of be and adjectives.

Wrap-up

  • Ask: “What new adjectives did you use today?”
  • Quick recap: Who / How old / What … like / What does he or she do?
  • Optional homework: write the description in clean form and bring next class.

Unit 1 is almost complete – students can now talk about real people in their lives.

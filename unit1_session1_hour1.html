Objectives – Hour 1

  • Review the verb be in the present.
  • Use be to talk about names, countries and jobs.
  • Practice basic written sentences.
  • Write a short introduction about yourself and another person.

Warm-up

Think of one person you know.

Ask yourself:
• Who is this person?
• Where is this person from?
• What is their job?

Example:
"She is my friend. She is from Mexico. She is a designer."

Grammar: Verb Be – Forms

  • I am → I’m
  • You are → You’re
  • He is → He’s
  • She is → She’s
  • We are → We’re
  • They are → They’re
Examples:
I’m from Mexico.
She’s a teacher.
They’re students.

Negative & Questions

Negative:
I’m not, you aren’t, he isn’t, she isn’t, we aren’t, they aren’t.

Yes / No questions:
Are you Mexican? – Yes, I am. / No, I’m not.
Is she a guide? – Yes, she is. / No, she isn’t.

Structure:
Wh-word + be + subject + complement
Where are you from?

Guided Writing – About You

Model:
"Hello, my name is Laura. I’m from Mexico City and I’m Mexican. I’m a student. I’m not a teacher. I’m very happy to study English."
Now write your own introduction in your notebook:
• Your name
• Your country and nationality
• Your job or occupation
• One extra sentence about you

Guided Writing – Another Person

Model:
"This is my friend Daniel. He’s from Costa Rica and he’s Costa Rican. He’s an architect. He isn’t a student."
Write about a friend, classmate or family member:
• Their name
• Their country and nationality
• Their job
• One extra detail
